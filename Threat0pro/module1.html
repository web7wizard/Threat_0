TRAINING MANUAL: PHISHING DEFENSE

1.0 DEFINITION

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack where a criminal poses as a legitimate institution (bank, employer, service) to lure individuals into providing sensitive data such as personally identifiable information, banking details, and passwords.

2.0 THREAT TYPES

  • SPEAR PHISHING (Level 1.1)
    Highly targeted attacks sent to specific individuals or organizations. Attackers gather personal details beforehand to make the email appear trustworthy (e.g., an email from "HR" about "your" specific salary).
  • CLONE & E-COMMERCE (Level 1.2)
    Attackers create a nearly identical copy of a legitimate email or website (Typosquatting). They often manipulate URLs (e.g., amaz0n.com) to trick users into entering credit card details on fake checkout pages.
  • ACCOUNT COMPROMISE (Level 1.3)
    The result of a successful breach. Once credentials are stolen, the attacker gains unauthorized access. Immediate Incident Response (IR) protocols are required to limit damage.

3.0 MODULE OBJECTIVES

By completing this simulation, the operator will learn to:

  • Analyze email headers for sender spoofing and false urgency.
  • Identify fake URL structures and lack of HTTPS encryption.
  • Execute the standard Recovery Protocol: Disconnect -> Secure Credentials -> Report.
